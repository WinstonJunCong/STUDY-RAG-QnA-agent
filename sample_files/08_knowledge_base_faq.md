# Knowledge Base — FAQ

## What is the NovaDesk Knowledge Base?

The Knowledge Base is a self-service help center where you can publish articles, guides, and FAQs for your customers. It's hosted at `yourcompany.novadesk.io/help` and is publicly accessible by default (no login required).

## Can I make the Knowledge Base private?

Yes. You can restrict access so only logged-in customers can view articles. This is useful for internal SOPs or partner documentation. Configure this under **Knowledge Base → Settings → Visibility**.

## How are articles organized?

Articles are organized into **Collections** (top-level categories) and **Sections** (subcategories within a collection). For example:

- Collection: "Getting Started"
  - Section: "Account Setup"
  - Section: "Billing"

## Can I have multiple Knowledge Bases?

Yes. Growth and Enterprise plans support **multiple knowledge bases** — useful if you serve different product lines or customer segments. Each knowledge base has its own URL and branding.

## Does the Knowledge Base support multiple languages?

Yes. You can create translated versions of articles for different locales. NovaDesk does not auto-translate — you write and maintain each language version manually.

## Can customers search the Knowledge Base?

Yes. There is a full-text search bar at the top of the help center. Search results are ranked by relevance using keyword matching and view count.

## Does the AI Bot use the Knowledge Base?

Yes. The AI Bot (available on Growth and Enterprise plans) uses your published Knowledge Base articles to automatically answer customer questions in chat and email before escalating to a human agent.

## How do I track article performance?

Go to **Knowledge Base → Analytics** to see:
- Article views and unique visitors
- Helpfulness ratings (customers can vote 👍 or 👎)
- Search terms that led to no results (great for identifying content gaps)

## Can I export Knowledge Base articles?

Yes. Go to **Knowledge Base → Settings → Export** to download all articles as a ZIP file of Markdown or HTML files.
